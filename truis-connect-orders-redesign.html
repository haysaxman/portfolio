Truis Connect Renewals Redesign

Truis Connect is a customer portal used by customers and staff of Truis to track and manage their orders, assets, renewals and health of their systems. After Truis rebranded, and with progress being made towards an e-commerce platform, Truis saw it was a priority to update their orders page on Truis Connect. This brought it in-line with their new look, providing a better overall user experience as their e-commerce release brings more traffic to the portal. The target audience are IT Managers of small to medium sized businesses who are typically people who are the first point of contact for our business and any deals made. My role in the project is a UX/UI Designer as well as a Front-end Developer. The challenge with this project was our lack of human resources, therefore I had to take on two roles during the project lifecycle.

Interviews

I started the project with interviewing stakeholders such as management, customer support and also regular users of the existing Orders page. I contacted around 12 customers and managed to organise four interviews with customers who have used the platform before. Unfortunately I had to wait a couple weeks between organising and conducting the interviews, so in the mean time I began the low fidelity designs.

Low fidelity designs

I started sketching ideas that were highlighted during staff interviews for the existing page. This allowed me to improve existing concepts, as well as the hierarchy and layout to provide better user experience. I constantly got feedback from the stakeholders and iterated on the designs further. I had enough time before the interviews were scheduled that I began to design higher fidelity designs in Figma.

High fidelity designs

Using a Bootstrap template in Figma I quickly made high quality renders of my sketches which looked closer to the end product. With my experience with Figma it was easy to set up components to allow for quick adjustments to multiple elements at a time. These designs were continuously improved on from the stakeholder input.



Persona

Conveniently, we already had a persona completed by the Marketing Team around our target audience (IT Managers), thus I used this research as the majority of customer portal users was this target audience.

Interaction map

I created an interaction map to better understand the flow of the user interactions to help streamline and to make sure the prototype accounts for everything.



Interactive Prototype

Using the high fidelity prototype created in Figma I was easily able to put together an interactive prototype that stakeholders could test to get more in-depth feedback.

Outcomes and lessons

Post release feedback was received well and got only a small amount of suggestions for improvement. This was mostly around adding more data fields and changing how the statuses worked based on customer service processes. Unfortunately, there was a lack of tracking and quantitative feedback data. To rectify this, I tried looking on Google analytics for valuable insights, but because Truis Connect has no home page and first loads the Orders page, the data was skewed. If I were to do this project again with more time and resources, I would have researched more upfront before commencing the design process, interviewed more customers and also conducted testing with users that weren't already stakeholders. Overall, this page design was a big step forward for the user experience design for Truis Connect to align it with the new brand.
